Grammar Directive 01

The Second Conditional

"If I were you, I would sever my memories."

Pedagogical Approach:

Explain that we use the Second Conditional for situations that are imaginary, unlikely, or impossible in the present or future.

Structure: If + Past Simple, ... would + Verb (infinitive)

Grammar Directive 01

Concept Checking Questions (CCQs)

Use these questions to ensure students understand the hypothetical nature of the grammar.

"If Mark didn't sever his memories, he wouldn't survive."

  • Q:
    Did Mark sever his memories?
    > Yes.
  • Q:
    Is he surviving right now?
    > Yes.
  • Q:
    Are we talking about the real past or an imaginary situation?
    > Imaginary situation.

Grammar Directive 01

Oral Drills

Have the students complete these sentences out loud.

  1. If Lumon [to offer] me the job, I [not / to take] it.
  2. If I [to be] an Innie, I [to try] to escape.
  3. She [to be] happier if she [to know] her Outie.
Show Answer Key
1. offered / wouldn't take.
2. were / would try.
3. would be / knew.

Grammar Directive 02

The Passive Voice

Corporate environments use the Passive Voice to remove human agency and sound more authoritative. It sounds like "the system" is doing it, not a person.

Active (Human)

"The doctors severed his memories."

Passive (Corporate)

"His memories were severed."

Grammar Directive 02

Transforming the Rules

Ask students to transform these active Lumon rules into the Passive Voice. Click to reveal answers.

1. The managers do not allow outside communication.

> Outside communication is not allowed.

2. The board monitors all employees.

> All employees are monitored.

3. The company holds a waffle party every quarter.

> A waffle party is held every quarter.
